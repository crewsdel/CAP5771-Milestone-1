We evaluated two models, Linear Regression and Random Forest, to predict weekly respiratory hospital admissions.
Linear Regression performed poorly, with a negative R-squared value of -0.88, indicating that it was not able to capture the relationship between AQI and admissions effectively.
This suggests that the relationship is not purely linear.

The Random Forest model performed significantly better, achieving an R² of about 0.59 and much lower error values: about 173 and 360, respectively (MAE and RMSE).
This indicates that the model was able to capture more complex, nonlinear patterns in the data.

From these results, we concluded that air quality does have predictive value for respiratory admissions, but the relationship is influenced by multiple factors and time-based effects. Overall, Random Forest was the better model choice for this problem
